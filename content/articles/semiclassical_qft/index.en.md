## Introduction
The principle of the deferred measurement 


We give the explicit proof for product states:
$$
 \ket{\Psi}  = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \ket{\psi_k}
$$
where
$$
\ket{\psi_k} = \alpha_k \ket{0} + \beta_k \ket{1} \qquad \qquad \alpha_k, \beta_k \in \mathbb{C}
$$

so 

$$
\ket{\Psi} =  \sum_{j_1=0}^{1} \dots \sum_{j_n=0}^{1} \alpha_1^{j_1} \beta_1^{1-j_1} 
\dots 
\alpha_n^{j_n} \beta_n^{1-j_n}
\ket{j_1, \dots, j_n} = \sum_{j_1=0}^{1} \dots \sum_{j_n=0}^{1} 
\prod_{i=1}^{n}\alpha_i^{j_i} \beta_i^{1-j_i} 
\ket{j_1, \dots, j_n}
$$
Now we apply the QFT^-1
$$
\text{QFT}^{-1} \ket{\Psi} = 
\sum_{j_1=0}^{1} \dots \sum_{j_n=0}^{1} 
\bigg(\prod_{i=1}^{n}\alpha_i^{j_i} \beta_i^{1-j_i}\bigg) 
\text{QFT}^{-1} \ket{j_1, \dots, j_n} = 
\sum_{j_1=0}^{1} \dots \sum_{j_n=0}^{1} 
\frac{1}{\sqrt{2^n}} 
\bigg(\prod_{i=1}^{n}\alpha_i^{j_i} \beta_i^{1-j_i}\bigg) 
\bigotimes_{l=1}^{n}  ( \ket{0}+ e^{2\pi i j2^{-l}} \ket{1} )
$$
recall that
 $$
0.j_{1} \dots j_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n} j_i 2^{-i}
 $$
 and
 $$
j = \sum_{i=1}^{n} j_i 2^{n-i}
 $$
Let's redefine 
$$
F_h(\alpha_h, \beta_h,j_h) := \alpha_h^{j_h} \beta_h^{1-j_h} 
$$
and 
$$
F(\ket{\Psi}, j_1, \dots ,j_n) := \prod_{h=1}^{n} F_h(\alpha_h, \beta_h, j_h) =\bigg(\prod_{i=1}^{n}\alpha_i^{j_i} \beta_i^{1-j_i}\bigg)
$$
Let's suppose we want to find the amplitude related to the 
output state $\ket{k_1, \dots , k_n}$
We can express the exponential amplitude of that state as a productory with a 
coefficient weighted on the bits we select
$$
\prod_{l = 1}^{n}e^{2\pi i j 2^{-l} k_l} =
 \prod_{l = 1}^{n}e^{2\pi i ( \sum_{i=1}^{n} j_i 2^{n-i}) 2^{-l} k_l} =
e^{2\pi i ( \sum_{i=1}^{n} j_i 2^{n-i}) \sum_{l=1}^{n} 2^{-l} k_l}
$$
so the final amplitude for that state is
$$
\sum_{j_1=0}^{1} \dots \sum_{j_n=0}^{1}  F(\ket{\Psi}, j_1, \dots ,j_n) e^{2\pi i ( \sum_{i=1}^{n} j_i 2^{n-i}) \sum_{l=1}^{n} 2^{-l} k_l} = 
\sum_{j_1=0}^{1} \dots \sum_{j_n=0}^{1} \prod_{h=1}^{n} F_h(\alpha_h, \beta_h, j_h)  e^{2\pi i j_h 2^{n-h} \sum_{l=1}^{n} 2^{-l} k_l} 
$$
if we take oput the j productory and swap the symbols
$$
\prod_{h=1}^{n} \sum_{j_h=0}^{1} F_h(\alpha_h, \beta_h, j_h) e^{2\pi i j_h 2^{n-h} \sum_{l=1}^{n} 2^{-l} k_l} = \prod_{h=1}^{n} \alpha_h + \beta_h  e^{2\pi i 2^{n-h} \sum_{l=1}^{n} 2^{-l} k_l} = \prod_{h=1}^{n} \alpha_h + \beta_h  e^{2\pi i  \sum_{l=1}^{n} 2^{n-h-l} k_l} = \prod_{h=1}^{n} \alpha_h + \beta_h  e^{2\pi i  \sum_{l=n-h+1}^{n} 2^{n-h-l} k_l} =
\prod_{h=1}^{n} \alpha_h + \beta_h  e^{2\pi i \sum_{l=1}^{h} 2^{-l} k_{n-h+l}} =
\prod_{h=1}^{n} \alpha_h + \beta_h  e^{2\pi i 0.k_{n-h+1} \dots k_n}
$$
So the probability for the Z-measurement corresponding to $\ket{k_1, \dots , k_n}$
is 
$$
P(k_1, \dots, k_n) = \bigg|\prod_{h=1}^{n} \alpha_h + \beta_h  e^{2\pi i 0.k_{n-h+1} \dots k_n} \bigg|^2 = \prod_{h=1}^{n}  \bigg| \alpha_h + \beta_h  e^{2\pi i 0.k_{n-h+1} \dots k_n} \bigg|^2 
$$
